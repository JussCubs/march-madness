# March Madness Calendar & Tournament Structure

Complete guide to NCAA Division I Men's Basketball Tournament dates, structure, and key milestones.

## 2026 Tournament Schedule

### Selection & Seeding
- **Selection Sunday**: March 15, 2026 (6:00 PM ET - CBS)
- **Bracket Release**: Immediately following selection show
- **Selection Committee Lock**: March 14, 2026 (end of conference tournaments)

### Tournament Rounds

#### First Four (Dayton, Ohio)
- **Dates**: March 17-18, 2026
- **Games**: 4 games (2 play-in games for 16-seeds, 2 for at-large teams)
- **Venue**: University of Dayton Arena
- **TV**: TBS, truTV

#### First Round
- **Dates**: March 19-20, 2026 (Thursday-Friday)
- **Games**: 32 games
- **Format**: 64 teams → 32 teams
- **Sites**: 8 venues across the United States
- **TV**: CBS, TBS, TNT, truTV

#### Second Round  
- **Dates**: March 21-22, 2026 (Saturday-Sunday)
- **Games**: 16 games
- **Format**: 32 teams → 16 teams (Sweet 16)
- **Sites**: Same 8 venues as First Round
- **TV**: CBS, TBS, TNT, truTV

#### Sweet 16 (Regional Semifinals)
- **Dates**: March 27-28, 2026 (Thursday-Friday)
- **Games**: 8 games
- **Format**: 16 teams → 8 teams
- **Sites**: 4 regional venues
- **TV**: CBS, TBS

#### Elite 8 (Regional Finals)
- **Dates**: March 29-30, 2026 (Saturday-Sunday)
- **Games**: 4 games
- **Format**: 8 teams → 4 teams (Final Four)
- **Sites**: 4 regional venues
- **TV**: CBS, TBS

#### Final Four
- **Dates**: April 4, 2026 (Saturday - Semifinals)
- **Games**: 2 games
- **Format**: 4 teams → 2 teams
- **Site**: Phoenix, Arizona (State Farm Stadium)
- **TV**: CBS

#### National Championship
- **Date**: April 6, 2026 (Monday)
- **Game**: 1 game
- **Format**: 2 teams → 1 champion
- **Site**: Phoenix, Arizona (State Farm Stadium)
- **TV**: CBS

## Tournament Structure

### Field of 68
- **At-Large Bids**: ~36 teams
- **Automatic Bids**: 32 teams (conference tournament winners)
- **First Four**: 4 teams eliminated before "real" tournament
- **Final Field**: 64 teams in main tournament bracket

### Seeding Structure
- **Seeds 1-4**: Protected seeds, cannot meet before Elite 8
- **Seeds 1-16**: Traditional seed lines (1 highest, 16 lowest)
- **Regional Distribution**: Geographic considerations for travel

### Regional Names & Sites (2026)
1. **East Regional**: Boston, Massachusetts (TD Garden)
2. **West Regional**: Los Angeles, California (Crypto.com Arena)
3. **South Regional**: Memphis, Tennessee (FedExForum)
4. **Midwest Regional**: Chicago, Illinois (United Center)

## Selection Committee Process

### Selection Criteria (in order of importance)
1. **Quadrant 1 Wins**: Wins vs top competition
2. **Strength of Schedule**: Quality of opponents faced
3. **HEAD-to-HEAD**: Direct competition results
4. **Quality Losses**: Competitive losses to good teams
5. **Eye Test**: Overall team quality assessment
6. **NET Ranking**: Primary ranking metric
7. **Conference Performance**: Success in conference play
8. **Non-Conference Performance**: Early season evaluation
9. **Key Player Availability**: Injury considerations
10. **Momentum**: Recent performance trends

### Quadrant System
**Quadrant 1** (Best Wins):
- Home games: vs NET 1-30
- Neutral: vs NET 1-50  
- Away: vs NET 1-75

**Quadrant 2**:
- Home: vs NET 31-75
- Neutral: vs NET 51-100
- Away: vs NET 76-135

**Quadrant 3**:
- Home: vs NET 76-160
- Neutral: vs NET 101-200
- Away: vs NET 136-240

**Quadrant 4** (Bad Losses):
- All other games

### Bubble Teams Evaluation
**Factors Considered**:
- Quadrant 1 record
- Overall strength of schedule
- Non-conference strength of schedule
- Road/neutral court performance
- Performance vs teams under consideration
- Significant wins and bad losses
- Conference tournament performance

## Bracket Pool Strategy

### Popular Bracket Formats
- **Standard**: 1 point Round 1, 2 points Round 2, 4-8-16-32 points
- **Upset Special**: Bonus points for picking lower seeds
- **Confidence Pool**: Assign confidence points to each pick

### Optimal Strategies
- **Chalk**: Pick mostly higher seeds (safer, lower ceiling)
- **Contrarian**: Pick strategic upsets (riskier, higher ceiling)
- **Hybrid**: Mix of safe picks and calculated risks

### Key Upset Scenarios
- **12 vs 5**: Historically ~35% upset rate
- **11 vs 6**: Often competitive games
- **13 vs 4**: Occasional major upsets
- **14+ vs 3-**: Rare but bracket-breaking

## Betting Considerations

### Market Efficiency
- **Early Lines**: Less efficient, more opportunity
- **Close to Game Time**: Very efficient markets
- **Live Betting**: High variance, quick decisions needed

### Popular Bet Types
- **Point Spreads**: Margin of victory predictions
- **Totals (O/U)**: Combined score predictions
- **Moneylines**: Straight win/loss bets
- **Futures**: Tournament winner, region winners
- **Player Props**: Individual performance bets

### Key Factors for March
- **Single Elimination**: Higher variance than regular season
- **Neutral Sites**: No home court advantage
- **Short Preparation**: Limited time to prepare for opponents
- **Pressure Situations**: Experience matters more
- **Pace Changes**: Teams may alter normal tempo
